Biography

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Data Science at the University of North Texas. My research focuses on computer vision and machine learning, specifically aiming to enhance the safety, interpretability, and robustness of computer vision systems. I completed my PhD in 2024 at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County.

Current Research Areas

Privacy Enhanced Machine Learning ▼
Federated Learning Training models across decentralized devices without sharing raw data. Differential Privacy Adding noise to data to prevent leakage of individual information. Homomorphic Encryption Performing computations on encrypted data without decryption.
Federated Learning ▼
Privacy Preservation Data remains on local devices, reducing exposure risks. Communication Efficiency Techniques like model aggregation and compression minimize data transfer overhead. Heterogeneous Data Handling FL accounts for non-IID (independent and identically distributed) data across clients.
Large Language Modeling ▼
Privacy-Preserving LLMs Integrating techniques like differential privacy and federated learning to secure user data. Efficient Training & Inference Optimizing models for reduced computational costs via distillation, quantization, and pruning. Adaptation to Low-Resource Settings Enhancing LLMs for languages and domains with limited training data.
Data Mining ▼
Scalable Data Mining Algorithms for Big Data Investigate efficient algorithms that can process massive datasets in real-time or near real-time, focusing on scalability and computational efficiency. Explainable Data Mining Models Create interpretable models that provide clear insights into the mining process, making results more transparent and actionable, especially in critical fields like healthcare or finance. Anomaly Detection in Complex Data Develop new methods for detecting unusual patterns in large and noisy datasets. This can have applications in fraud detection, cybersecurity, or sensor data analysis.
Health Informatics ▼
Predictive Analytics in Healthcare Use data mining and machine learning to predict patient outcomes, such as disease progression, treatment responses, and readmission risks. Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS) Design and evaluate intelligent systems that assist healthcare providers in making clinical decisions using patient data. Natural Language Processing (NLP) in Health Informatics Apply NLP techniques to analyze clinical texts, such as physician notes, medical records, and research papers, to extract meaningful insights.
Cybersecurity ▼
Adversarial Machine Learning and Robustness Investigate the vulnerability of machine learning models to adversarial attacks, where small, carefully crafted perturbations in data can lead to incorrect predictions. Cyber Threat Intelligence and Predictive Analytics Use data mining, machine learning, and big data techniques to predict and identify emerging cybersecurity threats and attack patterns. Security for Internet of Things (IoT) Research the unique security challenges associated with the proliferation of IoT devices, including secure communication, data storage, and device authentication.
